Design velocity for horizontal suction runs commonly falls in the 8–15 m/s range in field practice (varies by reference and application). Vertical risers need a minimum of ~2.5 m/s to entrain oil back to the compressor — undersizing risers, not oversizing, is the real risk here.

Note — This calculator sizes refrigerant lines from first-principles (mass flow, target velocity, standard copper tube sizes) using typical published refrigerant properties at standard AC operating conditions — it is not tied to any specific manufacturer's proprietary piping design software or capacity-derating tables. Refrigerant piping design directly affects oil return and compressor reliability, so treat this as a preliminary sizing pass: confirm final pipe sizes, riser oil-trap requirements, and long-line capacity correction against the equipment manufacturer's official piping design guide before installation.
