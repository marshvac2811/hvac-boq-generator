How this works — Section 02 solves for the duct diameter needed to hit your target velocity, or (Equal Friction method) the diameter that gives your target Pa/m at this airflow, using Darcy-Weisbach with the Swamee-Jain friction factor for air. It then rounds up to the nearest standard duct size and recalculates actual velocity and friction at that size. Equivalent diameter via the Huebscher equation (ASHRAE): De = 1.30 × (ab)^0.625 / (a+b)^0.25 — gives the round duct with equal friction loss and airflow capacity. Keep aspect ratio ≤ 4:1, ideally ≤ 3:1, to control pressure loss and sheet metal cost.
Air properties assumed: density 1.2 kg/m³, kinematic viscosity 1.5×10⁻⁵ m²/s (approx. at the fluid temperature entered above). Fitting head-loss uses the equivalent length (L/D) method — for critical runs, cross-check high-loss fittings (dampers, take-offs) against manufacturer loss coefficients.
